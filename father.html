Send Off Ceremony

Father Cyrille at his native Batie

Laura Tonta August 25, 2019 4 Comments

We are delighted to welcome Father Cyrille who has a vision of reaching out to all the people. Through his compassion and empathy for people, Father Cyrille began serving by simply observing that there was a void within his community to assist others in need. He has made significant and substantial contributions in changing people's lives.

"My greatest joy in serving is to see lives being changed for the better and people grappling with hard issues finding the strength to do so as they trust Jesus to keep his word, ‘I will never leave you or forsake you’ (Hebrews 13:5)."

Our Church is very much a local church, made up of local people. We are ordinary people, a gathered community who wants the world to be more the place God intended it to be, one where God is the focus, because then everything else just falls into place.

Une Cérémonie à retenir

Ceremonie qui débute à 9 heures 30 minutes, avec la participation des autorités religieuses évidement: le curé du village, l'évêque auxiliaire de Bafoussam, quatre prêtres de la congrégation Saint Jean. Les autorités administratives dont le maire de Batié, le délégué du gouvernement de Bafoussam, le sous Préfet. Les autorités administratives dont le chef supérieur Batié avec sa suite. Ceremonie elle même très imposante avec une grande chorale

Father Cyrille, preacher, teacher, and lecturer, is true to the calling that God has placed upon his life. He stands on the principle of God. While others are searching for economic plans for prosperity, he believes we only need to look to the word of God. “Give, and it shall be given to you; good measure, pressed down, shaken together, running over, will be poured in your lap.” Luke 6:38.

Many blessings have been bestowed upon Father Cyrille due to his commitment to spreading the word of God and remaining a humble servant. As an educator he seizes every opportunity to bring forth a lesson that will help his congregation grow spiritually as well as afford them the opportunity to learn more about Jesus and his rich history.

On the August 25th, Father Cyrille Bernard Tchemwe was received with great plomb at his native Batie/Djedem in the presence os family members, the Paramount Chief of Batie, the Bishop and many other well wishers sending him of to Conakry.
